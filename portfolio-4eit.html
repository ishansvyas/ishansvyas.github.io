Review of Electromagnetically Induced Transparency Cooling

Investigating a Cutting-Edge Technique in Enabling Quantum Computing

If you know Quantum Mechanics and the basics of Quantum Computing, skip to the full report below.

Simple Summary

When atoms are cold, they are easier to work with. Hot atoms vibrate a lot, and can be difficult to control. Especially for things like quantum computing, we need very precise control over everything that happens, including every electron's energy level in the system. For that reason, scientists care a lot about making atoms cold. Unfortunately, refrigerators are not enough, so they came up with things like Doppler Cooling. Even that wasn't good enough, so they came up with a new method called Electromagnetically Induced Transparency (EIT) Cooling. EIT Cooling uses lasers to cool atoms down to a few microkelvins by taking advantage of interference at a quantum level. This report details why EIT Cooling works, the math behind it, and some applications of it. The report directly cites primary literature in the field.
